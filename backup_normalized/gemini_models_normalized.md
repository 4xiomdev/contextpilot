# Gemini Models (Normalized)

Gemini models offer advanced capabilities for multimodal understanding and generation, catering to a variety of developer needs from intelligent reasoning to fast, cost-efficient processing.
https://ai.google.dev/gemini-api/docs/models

## Model Naming and Versioning

Gemini models are released with specific version patterns to indicate their stability and intended use. This system includes stable, preview, latest, and experimental versions.
https://ai.google.dev/gemini-api/docs/models/gemini#model-versions
https://ai.google.dev/gemini-api/docs/models

*   **Stable**: Points to a specific, unchanging model version. These are recommended for most production applications.
    *   *Example*: `gemini-2.5-flash`
    https://ai.google.dev/gemini-api/docs/models/gemini#model-versions
    https://ai.google.dev/gemini-api/docs/models
*   **Preview**: May be used for production, typically has billing enabled, and might have more restrictive rate limits. Preview models are deprecated with at least 2 weeks' notice.
    *   *Example*: `gemini-2.5-flash-preview-09-2025`
    https://ai.google.dev/gemini-api/docs/models/gemini#model-versions
    https://ai.google.dev/gemini-api/docs/models
*   **Latest**: An alias that points to the most recent stable, preview, or experimental release of a specific model variation. This alias is hot-swapped with each new release, with a 2-week email notice before changes.
    *   *Example*: `gemini-flash-latest`
    https://ai.google.dev/gemini-api/docs/models/gemini#model-versions
    https://ai.google.dev/gemini-api/docs/models
*   **Experimental**: Not generally suitable for production. These models are released to gather feedback and provide early access to new updates. They are not stable, and endpoint availability is subject to change. Experimental models typically come with more restrictive rate limits.
    https://ai.google.dev/gemini-api/docs/models/gemini#model-versions
    https://ai.google.dev/gemini-api/docs/models

## Model Deprecations

For information regarding model deprecations, please refer to the dedicated Gemini deprecations page.
